IEGF Activates Global Movement to Empower People and Shape Nations

24 March 2026 | South Africa | For Immediate Release

The Informal Economy Global Forum (IEGF) proudly announces the official launch of its new global website, marking a significant milestone in its mission to Empower People and Shape Nations.

This launch introduces a dynamic digital platform designed to connect individuals, businesses, and communities to meaningful opportunities. It also formally unveils IEGF's Global Leadership Team, a collective of experienced, purpose-driven leaders committed to driving sustainable economic transformation across countries.

At the helm is Rochelle Pillay, Global President, whose vision and leadership continue to position IEGF as a powerful global movement. Supporting this vision is a strong executive team, including Maria Motsidisi Kgabo, Global Secretary General, who plays a critical role in global coordination and execution, and Darren Rajamany, Global Head of Legal and Compliance, ensuring governance, integrity, and regulatory alignment across all operations.

Driving strategic growth and partnerships is Anthea Jacobs, Global Head of Strategic Partnerships and Stakeholder Engagements, whose extensive experience strengthens IEGF's ability to connect with key stakeholders and unlock opportunities. Global expansion and outreach are led by Deveshnee Mariah, Global Head of Membership, ensuring the continued growth and reach of the movement.

Program execution and capacity development are spearheaded by Ferdie Jacobs, Global Head of Programs and Capacity Building, ensuring that IEGF's initiatives are impactful, structured, and scalable across regions. Supporting the organisation's digital ecosystem is Lorenzo Jenecker, Global Head of Technology, who leads the development of systems that enable access, connectivity, and innovation across the IEGF platform.

Driving Impact Through Programs

At the heart of this milestone is the rollout of the Market Access Program (MAP), a transformative initiative designed to move individuals and businesses from survival to sustainability.

Through structured pathways focusing on compliance, branding, market positioning, financial well-being and access to opportunities, MAP equips participants with the tools needed to thrive in today's economy.

IEGF is further strengthening its impact through the introduction of over 23 multi-sector programs, strategically designed to address critical areas of development, including:

  • •Market Readiness & Access
  • •Financial Stability
  • •Infrastructure and Technology advancement
  • •Job Creation
  • •Community Impact & Development
  • •Mentorship and guidance to support growth and ensure sustainability in systems
  • •Hybrid approach: face-to-face and online engagements

Together, these initiatives aim to bridge the gap between informal economic activity and structured, sustainable growth, ensuring that individuals and businesses are not left behind, but are positioned to participate meaningfully in local and global economies.

A Digital Platform for Global Connection

The newly launched website serves as a central hub where users can:

  • •Access programs and opportunities
  • •Connect with the Grassroots Impact Connects (IEGF Access Points)
  • •Explore partnerships
  • •Engage with a growing global network committed to economic empowerment

Message from the Global President

"This is more than a website launch, it is the activation of a global movement. We are building systems that recognise the power of people, especially those in the informal economy, and creating pathways for them to rise into sustainable participation. Through our Global Team and National Teams, our Market Access Program, and our sector-based initiatives, we are not just creating opportunities — we are building futures, strengthening communities, and shaping nations. This is our time to move from potential to impact, together."

Rochelle Pillay

Global President, IEGF

A Global Call to Action

As IEGF continues to expand its global footprint, the organisation calls on individuals, businesses, civil societies, and partners to be part of this journey.

Through collaboration, innovation, and a shared commitment to impact, IEGF is driving a new narrative, one where the informal economy becomes a powerful engine for sustainable development, creating one plan for national development.

Together, we are not just responding to challenges, we are building solutions, unlocking potential, and creating a future where people are empowered, communities are strengthened, and nations are shaped through collective action.
