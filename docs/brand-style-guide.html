10. Brand Voice Needed

Brand voice defines how IEGF communicates in writing. This should be configured in Canva's Brand Voice feature so all team content stays consistent.

Tone Attributes (Recommended)

Empowering

We uplift and enable. Focus on what communities can achieve, not what they lack.

Inclusive

Accessible language. Avoid jargon. Speak to informal economy workers directly, not about them.

Professional

Credible and trustworthy. We're an organisation that partners with governments and institutions.

Action-Oriented

Direct, clear CTAs. "Join us", "Get involved", "Partner with us" — not passive language.

Key Terminology

  • "Informal economy" — not "informal sector" (economy is broader and more accurate)
  • "Workers" or "participants" — not "beneficiaries" (agency, not charity)
  • "People-centred" — core descriptor, hyphenated, used consistently
  • "Grassroots" — used for community-level work and the GIC programme
  • "Structured development" — IEGF's approach to formalising pathways

Still Needed

  • Approved tagline / strapline for use across materials
  • Elevator pitch (30-second description for bios, about sections, social profiles)
  • Boilerplate paragraph for press releases and formal documents
  • List of words/phrases to avoid

16. Video & Motion Needed

Branded motion graphics for video content and presentations.

  • Video intro — 3-5 seconds. Logo animation with Navy-to-Green gradient sweep. For webinars, YouTube videos.
  • Video outro — 5-8 seconds. Logo + website URL + social handles. CTA to get involved.
  • Lower third — Speaker name/title overlay for webinars and interviews. Navy bar with white text.
  • Animated social post — Short looping animations for social media (stats, quotes, announcements).
  • Webinar holding screen — "Starting soon" / "We'll be right back" screens with brand styling.

Motion Guidelines

  • Easing: Match the website — cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) for smooth deceleration
  • Transitions: Fade and slide, no harsh cuts. Elements should feel like they flow in.
  • Colour in motion: Navy-to-Green gradient sweeps are the signature brand animation
  • Duration: Keep intros/outros under 5 seconds to respect viewer time

17. Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Use Navy for headings and authority
  • Use Green for primary CTAs and action
  • Use Dark Grey for body text
  • Maintain high contrast for accessibility
  • Use the Navy-to-Green gradient for accents
  • Keep Montserrat as the only typeface

Don't

  • Don't use Navy and Green at equal visual weight — one should dominate
  • Don't use pure black (#000) for text — use Dark Grey or Navy
  • Don't use Amber or Red for decorative purposes — only for system states
  • Don't mix other typefaces with Montserrat
  • Don't place light text on Light Grey backgrounds
  • Don't use the gradient as a text colour
